General Information

HIPI is designed to provide a reliable, efficient, and easy-to-use library for performing large-scale distributed image processing tasks within the Hadoop programming framework. HIPI was created by Sean Arietta, Jason Lawrence, Liu Liu, and Chris Sweeney in the Computer Graphics Group at the University of Virginia.

The development of HIPI was partially supported by the National Science Foundation through a Cluster Exploratory (CluE) grant IIS-0844416 ("Image Super-Resolution using Trillions of Examples") and with resources generously made available by IBM and Google.
